State Visibility

Clients of Essential Care have the right to

  • Choose their own caregiver
  • Terminate service at any time
  • Decide when to start services
  • Receive considerate and respectful care
  • Participate in service plan development
  • Receive notification of any authority of the health division to examine records.
  • Receive the limits of the service plan and program criteria
  • Receive written policies and procedures of agency pertaining to charges, reimbursements, determinations of service plan
  • Get in touch with Essential Care 24 hours a day at 702-487-1703
  • Call Nevada Health Care & Compliance at 486-6515 to express any concerns or complaints

Qualifications and requirements of Essential Care Caregivers

  • Fingerprints & background checks
  • Yearly TB test
  • Physical
  • Current CPR/basic first aid training
  • Complete a 16 hour training course with written and on the job instruction in the following areas: PCA scope of practice, body mechanics, safety & universal precautions, personal care & grooming, bowel & bladder, care of the home & personal belongings, nutrition, communication, client rights & confidentiality, aging & disabilities, elderly neglect & abuse, advance directives, recognizing and responding to emergencies including fires and medical emergencies, dealing with adverse/confrontational behavior, skin care.

Grievance Policy:

  • In the event of a complaint or grievance with Essential Care Nevada, Inc., clients may contact the Area Manager of Essential Care, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week at (702) 487-1703. The complaint or grievance will then be evaluated and documented, and a response, solution or, if appropriate, explanation as to why no action was necessary, will be provided to the client no later than 12 hours after initial complaint/grievance telephone call is made.
  • If the situation involves a particular Attendant, Administrator will notify the Attendant and take the appropriate action.
  • All grievances will be documented in both Client and Attendant folders
  • Attendance Policy
  • If an Attendant fails to show up to an assigned job, clients may contact the Area Manager of Essential Care, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week at 702-487-1703 and, if necessary, a substitute Attendant will be dispatched as soon as possible. The Administrator will discuss with Attendant their failure to timely show up to the assigned job. Administrator will then make a determination as to whether to terminate the employee or issue a written warning.
  • If an additional visit is required, the Area Manager will be notified and give permission and collect fees for services rendered.
