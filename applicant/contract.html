Caregiver Independent Contractor Agreement

THIS AGREEMENT is made effective this , by and between (hereinafter “Contractor”) and EssentialCare. (hereinafter “Company”).

IN CONSIDERATION of the mutual covenants, conditions and promises contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

1.0 SERVICES. Contractor shall provide the Company with the services specifically set forth below (hereinafter “Services”) during the term of this Agreement. Contractor shall also perform other services at such compensation as the parties may agree in writing during the term of this Agreement. The service shall be the marketing and sale of non-skilled in-home care to seniors for the company.

2.0 COMPENSATION. The Company shall pay Contractor as compensation the following: $ per hour or $ per day for live in. Compensation is paid on recorded and initialed verification by the Company client. In the event Contractor no longer performs services for Company, compensation will stop on date of client cancelation and/or resignation/termination, and Company is not liable for compensation from that date forward. All work for company is on an as needed basis and not considered permanent or long-term employment.

3.0 INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR. It is the intent of the parties that during the term of this Agreement, Contractor shall be an independent contractor, and nothing set forth herein shall be deemed or construed to render the parties as joint ventures, partners or employer and employee. Contractor, will not engage any other party to perform its duties in agreement without prior written consent of the Company. In the event that employees or agents, or contractors of Contractor shall also perform Services for the Company; they shall be bound by the provisions of this Agreement. Company shall not reimburse Contractor for any expenses incurred by Contractor under this agreement. Contractor shall have full responsibility to any and all taxes attributable to Contractor.

4.0 INDEMNIFICATION. Contractor shall indemnify, defend and hold harmless the Company and its officers, directors, and affiliates from and against any and all loses, liabilities, damages, costs, claims and expenses sustained or incurred by the Company as a result of any breach of this agreement by Contractor or its employees, agents or contractors; any claim made against the Company by a third party (including but not limited to any state, federal of local regulatory agency or organization), which arise from the acts of Contractor. Company shall have right to offset indemnification liability against any commission due to Contractor.

5.0 DURATION AND TERMINATION. This Agreement shall be effective from the date hereof and will continue in effect, unless earlier terminated as provided in this Agreement. This Agreement may be terminated as follows:

A. This Agreement is valid from client to client and is terminated when the client cancels service. Management may renew the agreement for another client but is not liable to provide work beyond this particular client.

B. Each Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon notice to the other (1) in the event of any material breach of this Agreement by the other party or (2) if the other party ceases to function as a going concern, files or has filed against it, any petition or action in bankruptcy or for the relief of creditors, or if the other party makes any assignment for the benefit of creditors or ceases to meet its financial obligations when due.

C. In the event that Contractor is given opportunity to receive compensation for service to other clients but declines opportunity not once, twice, but three times will be considered by Company as Contractor terminating relationship with Company.

D. Contractor will not discuss with Company client any self-promoting personal business ventures, money issues of any kind or personal family issues. Doing so wiii cause immediate self-termination. This is in direct violation of professional conduct.

7.0 GENERAL PROVISIONS. The indemnification obligations created herein shall survive any expiration or Termination of this Agreement.

7.01 ASSIGNMENT. Contractor shall not assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company.

7.02 GOVERNING LAW. It is agreed that this Agreement shall be governed by, construed and enforced in accordance with the laws of the State of Arizona.

7.03 ENFORCEMENT. In the event that either party initiates any legal proceeding to construe or enforce the terms and provisions of this Agreement or to obtain damages or other relief to which a party may be entitled by virtue of this agreement, the prevailing party shall be awarded its reasonable attorney fees by the other party.

7.04 ENTIRE AGREEMENT. This Agreement shall constitute the entire agreement between the parties, and any prior understanding or representation of any kind preceding the date of this Agreement shall not be binding upon either party except to the extent incorporated in this Agreement.

7.05 PARTIAL INVALIDITY. If any provision in this Agreement is held by a court of competent jurisdiction to be invalid, void, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions will nevertheless continue in full force with out being impaired or invalidated.

7.06 CONFIDENTIALITY/NON COMPETE. Contractor acknowledges that during the course of its relationship with Company it will receive and have access to certain clients and proprietary information. Contractor acknowledges that they are providing services to EssentialCare Clients and are or may be subject to agreement prohibiting Contractor from working, directly, or indirectly, for Client while employed by Essential Care, or in the event that Contractor ceases or terminates their working relationship with Essential Care. Accordingly, Contractor agrees not to be hired or otherwise permit services to be provided to EssentialCare Client by any past or present Client, directly or indirectly, during the term of their employment or thereafter for twelve (12) months from the date that the Contractor and Client terminates their relationship with Essential Care, in the county(s) of Maricopa and Pinal in the non-skilled home care industry within the scope of healthcare for Seniors. Contractor agrees not to engage in soliciting, or enticing any employee or other independent contractor of Company to leave Company or interfere adversely with Company’s employees and independent contractors. It is acknowledged that if Contractor breaches this obligation, Company will suffer great and irreparable injury in amounts difficult to ascertain. Therefore, Company shall be entitled to enforce the terms of this provision by filing for a temporary and permanent injunction in addition to any action for the recovery of damages sustained by Company.
